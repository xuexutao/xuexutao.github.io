3DHGS

3D Half-Gauusians, where each half can have different opacity values \(α_1\) and \(α_2\)

\[ HG_\Sigma(\mathbf{x}) = \begin{cases} e^{-\frac{1}{2} (x - \mu)^T \Sigma^{-1} (x - \mu)} & \mathbf{n}^T(x - \mu) \ge 0 \\ 0 &\mathbf{n}^T(x - \mu) \lt 0 \end{cases} \]

  • \(n = [n_1, n_2, n_3]^T\) is the normal of the splitting plane, represented as a column vector, while the other half Gaussian is obtained by changing the sign of the normal.

Pixelgs

\[ radius = 1.1 \times \max\limits_{i} \left\{ \left\Vert C_i - \frac{1}{N} \sum\limits_{j=1}^{N} C_j \right\Vert \right \} \]

scale the gradient of the NDC coordinates for each Gaussian \(i\) under the kth viewpoint, with the scaling factor \(f (i, k)\) being calculated by:

\[ f(i,j ) = \text{clip} \left ( \left ( \frac{\mu_{c,z}^{i,k}}{\gamma_{depth} \times radius } \right )^2 , 0, 1 \right ) \]

  • N viewpoints
  • \(C_j\) representing the coordinates of the jth viewpoint’s camera in the world coordinate system
  • \(\mu_{c,z}^{i,k}\) is the z-coordinate of Gaussian \(i\) in the camera coordinate system under the kth viewpoint,

The paper “Floaters No More” notes that floaters in NeRF are mainly due to regions close to the camera occupying more pixels after projection, which leads to receiving more gradients during optimization.

为什么f选平方？

The number of pixels occupied is inversely proportional(成反比) to the square of the distance to the camera, hence the scaling of gradients by the squared distance.

A major issue with pixel-based optimization is the imbalance in the spatial gradient field, leading to inconsistent optimization speeds across different areas. ( 基于像素的优化的一个主要问题是空间梯度场的不平衡，导致不同区域的优化速度不一致。所以引入的缩放\(f\) )

最终得到的公式：

\[ \frac{\sum\limits_{k=1}^{M^i} m_k^i \times f(i, k) \times \sqrt{ \left (\frac{\partial L_k }{\partial \mu_{ndc,x}^{i,k}} \right )^2 + \left (\frac{\partial L_k }{\partial \mu_{ndc,y}^{i,k}} \right )^2 }}{ \sum\limits_{k=1}^{m^i} m_k^i} > \tau_{pos} \]

Spectral-GS

Illustrations of the optimization and densification of Gaussians in 3D-GS;

  • Correct Gaussians: When viewpositional gradients ∇μproj L exceed a certain threshold τloss, 3DGS decides to clone or split based on the Gaussian’s spectral radius ρ (Σ).
  • Needle-like Gaussians: 3D-GS does not split or clone Gaussians with low spectral entropy but small gradients.

定义：谱半径(Spectral radius) \(Σ\) 的光谱半径: \(ρ (Σ) = max(s^2_1, s^2_2, s^2_3 )\) Spectral Radius: In mathematics, the spectral radius \(ρ (·)\) of a square matrix(方阵) \(A\) is the maximum of the absolute values of its eigenvalues: \[ρ (A) = max( | \lambda_1 |,| \lambda_2 |, ..., | \lambda_n| )\]

镨熵(Spectral entropy)：

\[ H(\Sigma) = tr(-\frac{\Sigma}{tr(\Sigma)} ln \frac{\Sigma}{tr(\Sigma)}) = - \sum_{i=1}^{3} \frac{s_i^2}{tr(\Sigma)} ln\frac{s_i^2}{tr(\Sigma)} \]

条件数(condition number)：

\[ \kappa(\Sigma) = \frac{\partial \Sigma}{\partial_{min}(\Sigma)} = \frac{max(s^2_1, s^2_2, s^2_3 )}{min(s^2_1, s^2_2, s^2_3 )} \]

这些度量可用于测量高斯各向异性的形状或程度

when \(s1 = s2 = s3\), the condition number is minimized and the spectral entropy is maximized.

FlashGS

original 3DGS rasterizer

分析：In some high-resolution, large-scale scenes, 3DGS can generate millions of or even more (huge) Gaussians. optimizing memory usage can be categorized into two main types:

  • Leveraging widely used techniques in model compression, like pruning and encoding
    • Scaffold-GS、EAGLES、LightGaussian
  • Introducing multi-GPU parallelism to avoid the memory constraints of a single GPU

Observation 1: Excessive redundant computations hinder efficient rendering

Geometry Redundancies. There are 3 kinds of redundancies in original 3DGS intersection algorithm:

  • The definition of ellipse ignores the opacity

实现采用 \(3\sigma\) 经验法则，即绝对差值超过 \(3\sigma\) 的数据点极为罕见，并使用 \(3\Sigma'\) 定义椭圆的边界,当不透明度足够低时，高估问题更加严重

\[ r = 3\sqrt{\lambda} \]

λ represents the semi-major axis length of the projected ellipse, which corresponds to the eigenvalue of the covariance matrix

r is the radius of the circle abstracted from the ellipse.

  • The AABB is overestimated

AABB 比椭圆的真实 AABB 更大，特别是对于高度扁平化的椭球体，这会为键值分箱、排序和渲染引入计算冗余。

  • The tiles out of the ellipse are binned with the Gaussian

The duplicatedWithKeys kernel 将 AABB 中的冗余图块分配给KV pairs

即使该图块完全没有被椭圆覆盖，AABB 中的所有图块都会被遍历并装箱为键值对，这将导致后续排序和渲染过程中的冗余计算。

Observation 2: Inappropriate execution pipelines reduce hardware utilization

The main steps in 3DGS exhibit an imbalance between computation and memory access, which can put excessive pressure on the corresponding hardware units, causing computation or memory bottlenecks

memory

The preprocess step (preprocessCUDA in Algorithm 1) kernel has a linear complexity \(O (P)\) , where \(P\) is the number of Gaussians. And each Gaussian is related to a large array of spherical harmonics coefficients. This results in significant pressure on cache bandwidth due to the massive read-write operations (lines 17-22 of Algorithm 1)

During the rasterization phase, 3DGS first projects Gaussian spheres on tiles to obtain the tile-gaussian pairs (DuplicateWithKeys). Its asymptotic complexity depends on the number of Gaussians and the number of tiles covered by their projection \(O (P + N )\) . As shown in Algorithm 2, in this step, aside from some bitwise operations(按位于), all the operations involve reading and writing to global memory.

computation

During rendering, the extensive pixel-level calculations introduce computational bottlenecks,

FlashGS Design

optimize the Gaussian rendering process in two stages

  • design a more precise intersection algorithm to alleviate subsequent computational bottlenecks related to redundant Gaussian calculations, and use geometric simplifications to avoid expensive intersection computations.
  • reorganized and restructured the computation process based on the characteristics of each kernel/micro kernel to prevent unbalanced workloads

greatly benefits from the reduced number of false positive tile kv pairs in FlashGS, as well as from the optimization in algorithms and scheduling for runtime.

the precise intersection algorithm can reduce the number of Gaussians involved in subsequent computations, thereby alleviating the computational and memory access intensity/pressure. This is crucial because for the two vital bottlenecks, sorting and rendering, their workload and the number of Gaussians to be processed are proportional.

not only the effect of the general Gaussian distribution on the effective range but also the changes in opacity

\[ \alpha = \alpha_0 \times exp \left( - \frac{r^2}{2 \lambda} \right) \]

\(\alpha_0\) is the initial (central) opacity of the Gaussian

When \(\alpha\) decays to a certain threshold \(\tau\), consider this to be the boundary of the ellipse

\[ r = \sqrt{2 ln(\frac{\alpha_0}{\tau} ) \lambda} \]

typically \(τ= \frac{1}{255}\) RGB typically has 256 discrete values

Optimized the algorithm in two key aspects:

  • applying algebraic simplifications to reduce the overhead of a single tile-ellipse intersection;

  • proposing an adaptive scheduling strategy to balance the intersection tasks of the entire projected ellipse of a Gaussian.
